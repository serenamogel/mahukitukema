New Swiftie flex: Taylor Swift fans break Google in &quot;1989 (TV)&quot; promo

June 2024 Â· 2 minute read

Taylor Swift has once again sent her legion of devoted fans, affectionately known as Swifties, on a wild virtual treasure hunt as she teased the release of her upcoming album, "1989 (Taylor's Version)."

This time, she partnered with Google for an interactive puzzle challenge, but things haven't gone entirely as planned.

Watch Taylor Swift's sneak peek of '1989' video

Swift posted the challenge on her Instagram page with a 13-second video that she captioned with "You can tell me when the search is over... if the high was worth the pain."

Swifties made Google 'glitch'

The puzzles began with a scrambled version of the album name and grew progressively more complex as participants entered answers into the search bar. Hints such as "I Love You," "All You Had to Do Was Stay," "Karma Music Video," and "Likes, Job, Whereabouts Were Studied Intently" were sprinkled across the different puzzles.

One intriguing revelation from the puzzle quest was the appearance of the astrological sign Sagittarius, sparking speculation that Nicki Minaj might be featured on the album.

However, the complexity and sheer number of puzzle completions "broke" the activation on Google search forcing the tech giant to release a statement on X letting fans know they were indeed experiencing problems due to the high demand of searches in the platform saying "Swifties, the vault is jammed! But don't worry, there are no blank spaces inside. We're in our fix-it era and will be out of the woods soon"

Despite the hiccups, Taylor Swift continued to keep her fans engaged. Unsurprisingly, searches for "Taylor Swift" surged throughout the day, demonstrating the unwavering dedication of Swifties to uncover the secrets behind "1989 (Taylor's Version)."

Those fans who were able to play the word scramble while the game was down didn't take long to figure out all 89 puzzles and quickly shared them among the fandom to speculate on possible track names.

While Swifties wait for the singer's next bait and switch, they still collectively have to complete the assigned task of solving 33 million puzzles, so their quest is not over yet
