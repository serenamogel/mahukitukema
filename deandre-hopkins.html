DeAndre Hopkins Career:

After his freshman season, in the 2010–11 season, DeAndre played basketball for the Tigers basketball team in seven games. DeAndre left Clemson with a career record for 3020 receiving yards and 27 career touchdown receptions. On 10th January 2013, he decided to forgo his senior year season and enter the NFL Draft.

On 7th March 2013, DeAndre participated in Clemson’s pro day and attended private visits with the Carolina Panthers, Dallas Cowboys, New England Patriots, and St. Louis Rams. In the 2013 NFL Draft, Hopkins was picked in the 1st round by the Houston Texans with the 27th overall pick.

On 24th July 2013, he signed a four-year contract worth $7.62 million. The first 3 years of the contract were fully guaranteed. He made his NFL debut in the season opener and made 5 receptions for 55 yards against the San Diego Chargers in the 31–28 victory. During this season for September month, DeAndre was named the NFL Rookie of the Month. 

Further, he finished the 2013 season scoring 52 receptions for 802 receiving yards and 2 touchdowns. For the 2013 season, DeAndre was named to the NFL All-Rookie Team.

To begin the 2014 season DeAndre under new head coach Bill O’Brien returned as a starting wide receiver alongside Andre Johnson. During this season, he was the Texans’ leading receiver in 16 games and 16 starts; he recorded 76 receptions for 1,210 yards and 6 touchdowns. 

Then to begin the 2015 season, DeAndre was consolidated as the Texans’ number-one receiver after veteran Andre Johnson lost to the Indianapolis Colts in free agency. DeAndre finished the season by recording then-career highs in receiving yards (1,521), receptions (111), and touchdowns (11). He earned his 1st career Pro Bowl nomination and was named 2nd-team All-Pro.

Additionally, on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2016, he ranked 19th among his fellow players. In the 2016 season, DeAndre had 78 receptions for 954 yards and 4 touchdowns; it was his lowest total since his 2013 rookie season.

On 31st August 2017, the Houston Texans signed DeAndre to a five-year contract extension worth $81 million with $49 million guaranteed and $7.5 million as a signing bonus. Further, DeAndre finished the 2017 season scoring 96 receptions for 1,378 yards and 13 touchdowns. He led the team in scoring 78 points among wide receivers. For the 2017 season, he was named a First-team All-Pro and was ranked 13th by his team players on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2018.

In the 2018 season, DeAndre finished recording career-high records like 115 catches, 1,572 receiving yards, and 11 touchdowns. In addition, he finished 3rd in the league in receptions and 2nd in receiving yards. He was also named to his 3rd Pro Bowl and his 2nd straight First-team All-Pro. The team won the AFC South with an 11–5 record and achieved the #3 seed for the AFC Playoffs. DeAndre recorded 5 receptions for 37 yards in the Wild Card Round, a game against the Indianapolis Colts; he played with a torn shoulder ligament. Further, on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2019, he was ranked 11th by his team players.

DeAndre finished the 2019 season recording 104 receptions for 1,165 receiving yards and 7 touchdowns.

Additionally, on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2020, he was ranked 8th by his fellow players. On 16th March 2020, the Texans traded DeAndre to the Arizona Cardinals for running back David Johnson. On 8th September 2020, DeAndre signed a two-year contract extension worth $54.5 million with the Cardinals.
